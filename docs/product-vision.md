## The Problem We're Solving

### The Democratic Disconnect

There's a growing chasm between Portuguese citizens and their elected representatives:

| Citizens feel... | Representatives face... |
|------------------|------------------------|
| Ignored between elections | Information overload |
| Unable to understand what's happening | No systematic way to gauge public opinion |
| Fooled by promises never kept | Pressure from organized lobbies, not citizens |
| Powerless to influence outcomes | Disconnect from everyday concerns |
| Governed by an out-of-touch political class | Voters who feel alienated |

**The result:** Low trust, low engagement, and a democracy that exists in name but not in practice.

### Why This Happens

1. **Information asymmetry** â€” Politicians know what's being discussed; citizens don't
2. **Complexity barrier** â€” Parliamentary processes are deliberately or accidentally opaque
3. **No feedback loop** â€” Citizens can't easily respond to what's being proposed
4. **Time poverty** â€” People have lives; they can't spend hours researching legislation
5. **Learned helplessness** â€” "My opinion doesn't matter anyway"

## Our Mission

**Make parliamentary activity accessible, understandable, and actionable for every Portuguese citizen.**

We believe that an informed citizenry is the foundation of a healthy democracy. When people understand what their representatives are doing, they can:
- Hold them accountable
- Provide meaningful input
- Make informed voting decisions
- Participate in public debate with facts, not just feelings

## Core Principles

### 1. Radical Transparency
Everything public should be publicly accessible. No paywalls. No registration barriers. No gatekeeping.

### 2. Political Neutrality
Viriato presents facts, not opinions. We don't tell citizens what to think â€” we give them the information to think for themselves.

### 3. Citizen-First Design
Every feature asks: "Does this help a regular citizen understand and engage?" If not, it doesn't belong.

### 4. Accessibility Over Completeness
Better to explain 80% clearly than 100% confusingly. Progressive disclosure: simple first, details on demand.

### 5. Open Source, Open Data
Democracy belongs to everyone. Our code and our processed data are public goods.

## Why "Viriato"?

Viriato was a legendary Lusitanian leader who united tribes against Roman occupation in the 2nd century BC. He represents:

- **Resistance** against distant, unaccountable power
- **Unity** of common people for a shared cause
- **Portuguese identity** rooted in self-determination
- **The underdog** who proved that organization beats resources

Today's challenge isn't a foreign army â€” it's alienation from our own democratic institutions. Viriato aims to help citizens reclaim their democracy, not through revolution, but through **information, understanding, and participation**.
